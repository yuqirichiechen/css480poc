Things to Read

Articles, papers, tools, and rabbit holes I keep coming back to — across tech, markets, and design.

Links

Use Tab / Shift+Tab to move between links, or press ↑ ↓ arrow keys when the list is focused. Press Enter to open a link.

  • Design Nielsen Norman Group — Articles The gold standard for UX research and usability writing. Covers everything from cognitive load to mobile patterns.
  • AI Anthropic Research Primary AI safety and capability research from the team building Claude. Dense but worth it.
  • HCI arXiv — Human-Computer Interaction Preprint papers in HCI, interaction design, and accessibility research before they hit conferences.
  • Tech Paul Graham — Essays Sharp, opinionated writing on startups, programming, and how to think. A classic for a reason.
  • Finance Investopedia — Financial Terms Go-to reference for financial concepts, from options Greeks to macroeconomic indicators.
  • Dev CSS-Tricks Deep dives into CSS, layout, and front-end techniques. The flexbox and grid guides are invaluable.
  • A11y WebAIM — Accessibility Articles Practical, no-nonsense guides to web accessibility, WCAG compliance, and keyboard navigation.
  • Business Harvard Business Review — Technology Where tech intersects management, strategy, and leadership. Good for understanding the business side of what we build.
  • Dev roadmap.sh Visual, community-driven roadmaps for learning full-stack dev, DevOps, and everything in between.
  • News Morning Brew Business and tech news written to actually be readable. Good daily habit for staying current.
  • Career LeetCode — Interview Experiences Real candidate accounts of technical interviews at major companies. Useful prep and reality check.

Keyboard Access

Every link on this page is fully keyboard accessible. Here is how to navigate:

KeyAction
TabMove forward through nav links and reading list links
Shift + TabMove backward through interactive elements
↑ / ↓ Arrow keysMove between reading list links when the list is focused
Home / EndJump to the first or last reading list link
EnterOpen the focused link in a new tab

The key usability principle applied here is logical focus order — the Tab sequence follows the visual reading order top to bottom, so keyboard users never have to guess where focus will land next. All links open in a new tab so you never lose your place on the reading list.
